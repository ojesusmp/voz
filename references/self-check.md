# Self-check before delivering

Run this on anything longer than a sentence or two. It takes about twenty seconds and catches almost everything. The goal is not a perfect score; it is to break the patterns that make text read as machine-written.

## The pass

1. **Kill-list words.** Scan for the usual suspects: delve, intricate, tapestry, testament, underscore, boasts, pivotal, leverage, robust, seamless, vibrant, foster, showcase, crucial, elevate, navigate, realm. Each one: replace it, or keep it on purpose because it is genuinely the most precise word and the rest of the text is clean.

2. **Stock phrases.** "It's important to note", "in today's fast-paced world", "plays a significant role", "when it comes to", "more than just", "a testament to". Cut them.

3. **Rule of three.** Any list of exactly three parallel adjectives or phrases that you wrote on reflex? Make it two, or four, or one. Vary it across the piece.

4. **Negative parallelism.** Any "It's not X, it's Y" or "not just X but Y"? Keep at most one per long piece, and only if it genuinely earns the drama. Otherwise rewrite as a plain statement.

5. **Inflated copulas.** "serves as", "stands as", "represents", "boasts", "features" where "is" or "has" is the truth. Swap them back.

6. **Significance tails.** Sentences ending in an "-ing" clause that asserts importance ("...cementing its role as..."). Cut the tail.

7. **Em dashes.** Search for them. Remove every one (house rule). Replace with a comma, colon, parentheses, or two sentences.

8. **Formatting.** Is every bullet list earning its place, or did you chop a paragraph into fragments? Are headings in sentence case? Any stray bold on ordinary words? Any emoji that does not belong?

9. **Rhythm.** Look at sentence lengths. All the same? Break the pattern: add a short one, let one run long.

10. **Opening line.** Read it cold. Does it say something, or clear its throat? If it only announces the real first sentence, delete it.

11. **Register.** Is this the right voice for this reader? A contract is not chatty; a Slack reply is not a legal brief; a landing page is not a spec.

12. **Say it aloud.** Read the whole thing in your head as if speaking to the person. Any sentence you would never say out loud gets rewritten.

## The one-question version

If you only have time for one check, ask: **would I say this, in these words, to this person, out loud?** If no, rewrite. That single test catches most AI stiffness on its own.

## What this is not

Not a detector pass. You are not scoring the text against a tool or trying to beat a classifier. You are making it clear, honest, and easy for a real reader. If it is that, it reads as human, because it is.
